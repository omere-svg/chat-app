# The Solar System

The Solar System formed about 4.6 billion years ago from a giant molecular cloud.
The Sun contains 99.8% of the system's total mass.

## Planets

Mercury is the smallest planet and the closest to the Sun. It has no moons and almost
no atmosphere, so its surface temperature swings from about 430°C in daylight to
-180°C at night.

Venus is the hottest planet, with an average surface temperature near 465°C, because
its thick carbon-dioxide atmosphere traps heat in a runaway greenhouse effect.

Earth is the only planet known to support life. About 71% of its surface is covered by
water, and it has a single natural satellite, the Moon.

Mars is known as the Red Planet because iron oxide (rust) covers its surface. It has
two small moons, Phobos and Deimos, and the tallest volcano in the Solar System,
Olympus Mons.

Jupiter is the largest planet — more than twice as massive as all the other planets
combined. Its Great Red Spot is a storm that has raged for at least 350 years.

Saturn is famous for its prominent ring system, made mostly of ice particles with a
smaller amount of rocky debris.

## Beyond the planets

Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006 by the International Astronomical
Union, because it has not cleared its orbital neighbourhood of other debris.
